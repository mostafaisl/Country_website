Brancoveanu Castle

Located in the heart of Romania, Brancoveanu Castle is a historic site famous for its stunning architecture. It was built between 1698 and 1702 by Constantin Brâncoveanu. The palace bears the name of the widow of the Romanian boyar Mogoș, who owned the land it was built on.

Painted Monasteries of Northern Moldova

These eight churches of northern Moldavia, built from the late 15th century to the late 16th century, their external walls covered in fresco paintings, are masterpieces inspired by Byzantine art. They are authentic and particularly well preserved.

Peleș Castle

One of Romania's most beautiful castles, located in the Carpathian Mountains, Peleș is a symbol of royal history. Peles became the first castle in Europe fully powered by locally produced energy. The castle was completed in 1914, and it remained a royal residence until 1947.
